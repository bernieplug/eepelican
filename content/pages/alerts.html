Hijack Alerts

Find out what those alerts mean






If you have recently received a hijack alert from Efficient Era and would like to know what that means and what you can do about it, here are some insights that can help you.

If you think you received an alert in error, please be sure to contact us at: support@efficientera.com with a copy of the alert and any details that can help us troubleshoot.

Types of Hijack Alerts and What They Mean

EXPLANATION

The terms Seller SKU, Mfr Part Number and Model Number are often used interchangeably within Amazon’s listing. Not all ASINs have the same layout for listing detail pages, so these attributes may not be visible to you. However, we have detected that these have been changed in Amazon’s database – they could have been initiated you, Amazon themselves, or a hijacker.

OUR RECOMMENDATION

Check the ‘Vital Info’ and ‘Offer’ tabs of your listing to confirm any changes you might have made. If your contributions for these fields doesn't match the current Amazon values, someone else may be overriding your listing. If you are not brand registered for this product, it may be another seller. If you are brand registered, Amazon internal teams may still override your contributions to the listing. Check if new values match the SKU of any competitors, check for new sellers who might be hijacking your listing, and be on the lookout for further listing changes. If you're brand registered, contact Amazon Seller Support and report the incorrect change to your product, with proof that this is one of your brand registered products, and the correct values you would like Amazon to restore.
